The slow swing drill — balls 1 to 20
  • First 5 balls at 50% effort only. So slow you can feel exactly what the clubface is doing through impact. This is deliberate — you're learning the sensation of the face closing, not trying to hit it far.
  • Feel the forearms rotating through impact — like turning a door handle to the left as you swing through. That rotation is what closes the face.
  • Aim right of target. This lets you swing freely to the right which keeps the path in-to-out and gives the face rotation somewhere useful to go.
  • Gradually increase to 65% across the next 15 balls — only add speed when the face feels like it's closing. If it starts opening again, drop back to 50%.
  • One swing thought only: forearms rotate left, swing right.
Build up — balls 21 to 40
  • Now 70% maximum. Setup checklist before every ball. One practice swing. Commit.
  • The only thought: feel the forearms closing the face. Not the path, not the elbow, not the finish. Just the forearms rotating.
  • Bad shot — step back. Tee height and grip are the two most common causes. Recheck both.
  • Good shot — note exactly what you felt. Try to replicate that feeling on the next ball immediately.
