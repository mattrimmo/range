Your best 9 iron shots carry 26 yards further than your worst — on virtually the same club speed. The difference isn't how hard you swing. It's where you catch the ball. A fluid swing that finds the centre of the face every time beats a hard swing that catches it thin or off the toe. Today is about building a tempo that lets your body find the sweet spot consistently — not about hitting it harder.
🎵 The 3-1 Tempo Framework
Tour players consistently swing at a 3:1 backswing to downswing ratio — three counts back, one count through. This is the rhythm that creates lag, builds power naturally, and stops the lash. Use this as your mental metronome throughout today.
ONE · TWO · THREE — GO
Back on 1, 2, 3 — then smooth through on GO
The key feeling: the backswing should feel unhurried. If you feel like you're going too slow — you're probably at the right speed. If the transition feels rushed — that's the lash. Pause at the top for one beat before firing.

The feel to build
  • 1️⃣
    Grip check before every ball. 2.5 knuckles, V's to right shoulder. Non-negotiable for every phase today.
  • 🎵
    Say the count aloud if it helps — one, two, three, go. The backswing is the three counts. The downswing is the one count. If your downswing feels slow — it's correct.
  • 🎯
    50–60% effort only. You are not trying to hit these far. You are loading a feeling. Contact quality not carry distance is the measure.
  • 👋
    Right shoulder under and through on every swing. Elbow tuck on the way down. Divot after the ball.
What good feels like
The ball should feel like it jumps off the face rather than being struck — a compressed, solid sensation rather than a hit. At 50% effort a pure 9 iron will still carry 100+ yards. If you're carrying less than that consistently the tempo or contact is off, not the effort level.

The only swing thought
  • 🎵
    Three counts back, one count through. The backswing should feel longer than the downswing. That's the correct feeling. If the downswing feels rushed — the backswing wasn't long enough.
  • 🔄

The data case for 80%
At 88–93 mph your driver FTP averages 2.68° — practically straight. At 93+ mph it goes to 5.38° — that's 49 yards offline average. Swinging harder costs you 46 yards of direction. 80% effort produces 88–93 mph naturally. That's the target zone.
